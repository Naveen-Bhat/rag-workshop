## Course Description

This course introduces the fundamental concepts of linear algebra, including vectors, matrices, linear transformations, and eigenvalues. Linear algebra is one of the most widely used areas of mathematics, with applications in computer science, engineering, physics, economics, and data science.

Linear algebra provides the mathematical foundation for machine learning, computer graphics, quantum mechanics, and many other fields. The concepts learned in this course are essential for advanced study in mathematics and for practical applications in science and technology.

Students will develop both theoretical understanding and computational skills. By the end of the course, students will be able to work with matrices and vectors fluently and understand the geometric intuition behind linear transformations.

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
- Perform matrix operations and solve systems of linear equations
- Understand vector spaces and subspaces
- Compute determinants and understand their properties
- Find eigenvalues and eigenvectors
- Apply orthogonalization and least squares methods
- Understand linear transformations geometrically

## Topics Covered

### Module 1: Systems of Linear Equations (Weeks 1-2)
- Introduction to linear systems
- Gaussian elimination and row reduction
- Row echelon form and reduced row echelon form
- Matrix notation and operations

### Module 2: Vectors and Matrices (Weeks 3-4)
- Vector operations in R^n
- Matrix multiplication
- Matrix inverse
- Elementary matrices
- LU factorization (introduction)

### Module 3: Vector Spaces (Weeks 5-6)
- Definition and examples of vector spaces
- Subspaces
- Linear independence
- Basis and dimension
- Null space and column space

### Module 4: Linear Transformations (Week 7)
- Definition and properties
- Matrix representation of transformations
- Change of basis
- Geometric interpretations

### Module 5: Determinants (Week 8)
- Properties of determinants
- Cofactor expansion
- Applications: area, volume, invertibility
- Cramer's rule

### Module 6: Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors (Weeks 9-11)
- Definitions and examples
- Characteristic polynomial
- Diagonalization
- Applications in differential equations and Markov chains
- Complex eigenvalues

### Module 7: Orthogonality (Weeks 12-13)
- Inner products and norms
- Orthogonal sets and orthonormal bases
- Gram-Schmidt process
- Orthogonal projections
- Least squares approximation

### Module 8: Applications (Week 14)
- Principal Component Analysis (PCA) overview
- Linear algebra in machine learning
- Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) introduction

## Why Linear Algebra Matters

Linear algebra is essential for:
- **Machine Learning:** Neural networks, dimensionality reduction
- **Computer Graphics:** 3D transformations, rendering
- **Data Science:** Principal component analysis, regression
- **Engineering:** Signal processing, control systems
- **Physics:** Quantum mechanics, relativity

This course prepares you for CS301 (Machine Learning) and many other advanced courses.
