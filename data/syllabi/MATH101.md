## Course Description

This course covers the fundamental concepts of single-variable calculus, including limits, derivatives, and integrals. Students will develop mathematical reasoning skills and learn to apply calculus to solve problems in science, engineering, and other fields.

Calculus is the mathematical language of change and is essential for understanding the physical world. This course provides the foundation for further study in mathematics, physics, engineering, economics, and many other disciplines.

The course emphasizes both conceptual understanding and computational proficiency. Students will learn not just how to calculate derivatives and integrals, but why these operations are meaningful and how to apply them.

## Learning Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
- Understand the concept of a limit and evaluate limits
- Compute derivatives using various rules and techniques
- Apply derivatives to solve optimization and related rates problems
- Understand the relationship between derivatives and integrals (Fundamental Theorem)
- Evaluate definite and indefinite integrals
- Apply integration to calculate areas and solve applied problems

## Topics Covered

### Module 1: Limits and Continuity (Weeks 1-3)
- Introduction to limits
- Limit laws and techniques
- One-sided limits and limits at infinity
- Continuity and the Intermediate Value Theorem
- Introduction to the derivative as a limit

### Module 2: Derivatives (Weeks 4-7)
- Definition of the derivative
- Derivative rules: power, product, quotient
- Chain rule
- Derivatives of trigonometric functions
- Derivatives of exponential and logarithmic functions
- Implicit differentiation
- Related rates

### Module 3: Applications of Derivatives (Weeks 8-10)
- Extreme values and critical points
- Mean Value Theorem
- First and second derivative tests
- Optimization problems
- Curve sketching
- L'HÃ´pital's Rule

### Module 4: Integration (Weeks 11-14)
- Antiderivatives and indefinite integrals
- Riemann sums and definite integrals
- Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
- Substitution method
- Area between curves
- Applications of integration
